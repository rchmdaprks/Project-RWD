The First Roman Empire

The Roman Empire (Latin: Imperium Romanum; Koine Greek: Basileia ton Rhomaion), known as SPQR (Latin: Senatus Populusque Romanus; trans.: The Roman Senate and People) was an empire which conquers around Rome, Italy, and expanded from the Europe, Asia, or even Africa. The Roman Empire established since 27 BC until 476 AD by the first Roman Emperor, Augustus.

The Decline of Roman Empire

The Roman Empire divided into two empires, the Western Roman Empire, that conquered around Europe and Northern Africa, and Eastern Roman Empire, which also conquered the Western Asia and Middle East. The Eastern Roman Empire known as the Byzantine Empire.
